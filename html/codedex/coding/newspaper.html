BASEBALL; Attendance Continues Its Slow Slide

As Major League Baseball returns today from its All-Star Game break, most teams have shown a modest decline in attendance so far this season and there is concern that total ticket sales for the season might fall for the third consecutive year.

But it could be worse. Rich Levin, a spokesman for the commissioner's office, said attendance had improved since early in the season.
Attendance was down 8 percent, compared to last year, early in the schedule, but just past the midseason point, attendance was off by only 2.5 percent. That is less than the 6 percent decline in attendance from 2001 to 2002.

Commissioner Bud Selig said in Chicago this week that there were many reasons for the early-season slump. ''You had horrendous weather,'' Selig said. ''We had the war in Iraq that started for the first two or three weeks of the season. You've got the economy that's soft.
